# CommissionLens: Predicting Commission-Justified Alpha in Indian Equity Mutual Funds

## 1. Problem

Indian retail investors overwhelmingly buy the *regular* plan of a mutual fund, which embeds a
recurring distributor commission of roughly 0.5 to 1.5 percent per year relative to the *direct*
plan of the identical portfolio. The two plans hold the same securities under the same manager;
the only difference is cost, and over a long SIP that gap compounds into a material shortfall.

An investor should pay that commission only when the fund's active management is expected to
overcome it, that is when forward alpha exceeds the expense gap. No systematic, forward-looking
framework exists to make that call for an individual fund-quarter. CommissionLens builds one.

## 2. Target

For each fund and quarter we define the forward-looking net alpha:

```
net_alpha(t+1) = 4 * ( r_fund(t+1) - rf_q  -  beta_t * ( r_bench(t+1) - rf_q ) )  -  expense_gap
```

where `r_fund` and `r_bench` are the direct-plan and benchmark quarterly total returns, `rf_q`
is the quarterly risk-free rate, `beta_t` is CAPM beta from the trailing window, the factor of
four annualises the quarterly alpha, and `expense_gap` is the annual regular-minus-direct
expense ratio. The label is `commission_justified(t+1) = 1[net_alpha(t+1) > 0]`.

## 3. Features

Each fund-quarter row carries three blocks, all observable at quarter close.

- **Rolling risk and return** (trailing 36 months of monthly returns): annualised CAPM alpha,
  beta, Sharpe ratio, information ratio, volatility, tracking error, maximum drawdown.
- **Fund attributes**: regular expense ratio, expense gap, log AUM, portfolio turnover, manager
  tenure, fund age.
- **Macro regime**: RBI repo rate, CPI inflation, yield-curve slope, FII and DII net flows.

## 4. Data

The reference dataset is generated by a synthetic engine covering 220 funds over 2013-2023. Each
fund is assigned a latent skill that reacts to the macro regime, plus a beta and idiosyncratic
volatility; direct and regular NAV paths are simulated daily, with the regular plan trailing the
direct plan by the expense gap. This keeps the pipeline reproducible offline while preserving a
learnable signal. Live runs use the `mfapi.in` client and an RBI/NSDL macro loader in `data.py`.

## 5. Model: CommissionNet

The estimator is deliberately not a gradient-boosted ensemble. The data is a panel with an
explicit time axis and a forward-looking label, so the model is a sequence network: a two-layer
bidirectional GRU over the last eight quarterly feature vectors, a learned attention layer that
pools the timesteps into one embedding, and a shared trunk feeding a regression head (net alpha,
Smooth L1) and a classification head (commission-justified, BCE with positive-class weighting).
Training uses AdamW, gradient clipping, a plateau learning-rate scheduler, and early stopping on
a temporally held-out validation set. Regression targets are standardised so the two losses sit
on comparable scales.

## 6. Evaluation protocol

Splits are strictly temporal: the most recent 20 percent of quarters form the test set, and the
latest slice of the remaining quarters is the validation set. Regression is scored with RMSE,
MAE, and R^2; classification with AUC-ROC, F1, and precision at the top decile. Precision at the
top decile is the operational metric: of the funds the model is most confident about, what
fraction actually justify their commission next quarter.

Results below are from the default synthetic run (seed 7, 220 funds, 2013-2023). Regenerate
with `py main.py train`; numbers are read from `artifacts/metrics.json`.

| Split | RMSE  | R^2   | AUC-ROC | F1    | Precision@10% | Base rate |
|-------|-------|-------|---------|-------|---------------|-----------|
| Test  | 0.089 | 0.157 | 0.728   | 0.610 | 0.818         | 0.449     |

R^2 of 0.16 is appropriate rather than disappointing: a single quarter of realised alpha is
dominated by idiosyncratic noise, so no honest model explains most of its variance. The
operational metric is precision at the top decile, 0.818 against a 0.449 base rate: of the funds
the model is most confident about, 82 percent actually justify their commission next quarter,
nearly double the unconditional rate.

## 7. Explainability

SHAP `GradientExplainer` attributions on the classification logit, averaged over the attention
window, rank the model's input features. The five most predictive characteristics on the default
run are:

| Rank | Feature | Mean absolute SHAP |
|------|---------|--------------------|
| 1 | information ratio  | 0.0266 |
| 2 | Sharpe ratio       | 0.0207 |
| 3 | annualised alpha   | 0.0161 |
| 4 | tracking error     | 0.0140 |
| 5 | FII net flow       | 0.0133 |

The top four are all skill-persistence signals: whether next-quarter alpha will clear the
commission gap is driven mainly by how consistently the manager has generated risk-adjusted
excess return, not by the size of the gap itself, which is small and stable across funds. FII
net flow is the leading macro modulator, reflecting the regime dependence built into the panel.
Full ranking in `artifacts/shap_importance.csv`; chart in `artifacts/shap_importance.png`.

## 8. SIP back-validation

Two monthly-SIP strategies are compared over 2018-2023, both buying regular plans so the
comparison isolates selection skill from plan type: a naive investor spreading contributions
across every fund, versus a model-guided investor concentrating each quarter into the top-decile
funds by commission-justification score. The guided model is trained only on quarters that close
before the simulation window. Both are scored with the same dated-cashflow XIRR solver.

On the default run a monthly SIP of Rs 5,000 (Rs 300,000 invested over 60 installments) gives:

| Strategy      | XIRR   | Final corpus | Installments |
|---------------|--------|--------------|--------------|
| Model-guided  | 22.68% | Rs 526,219   | 60           |
| Naive regular | 19.51% | Rs 487,371   | 60           |

The commission-aware strategy earns a 3.17 percentage-point XIRR edge, roughly Rs 39,000 of
additional corpus on the same contributions, purely by concentrating into the funds the model
scores as commission-justified. Numbers in `artifacts/simulation.json`; chart in
`artifacts/sip_xirr.png`.

## 9. Limitations and future work

- The default dataset is synthetic; reported numbers validate the pipeline, not real funds.
- Net alpha annualises one quarter of return and holds beta fixed at the window estimate; a
  multi-quarter horizon and time-varying beta would reduce target noise.
- The macro block is coarse; sector exposures, factor tilts, and flow momentum are extensions.
- A walk-forward retrain every quarter would make the backtest fully causal at more compute cost.
